AI Statement

Transparency on our use of generative AI in development

Hi! My name is Albert, and I am the developer and visionary behind Mutate! Fight! Purr!. It’s nice to meet you!

Fully understanding the gaming community's stance on the use of generative AI in game development, I decided to create this page to clear the air before any questions arise.

1. We are not trying to shrink the team by replacing people with AI agents.

SandmanTeam consists of 3 people. Despite my passion for automation and professional pipelines, our previous game took 9 years to develop. When we started our new game, it was obvious that the old pipeline wouldn't work. Fortunately, AI has evolved enough to help us efficiently handle part of our tasks.

Our goal was never to reduce the team. On the contrary, as a team using AI, we can do much more than before. For us, it is simply the productivity tool we’ve been looking for, a chance to create the projects we truly love.

2. AI gives us the opportunity to maintain creative freedom.

We are a self-funded team. Our main resources are our own energy and sleepless nights. Hiring contractors for tasks that AI can handle would not only take more time but would also force us to seek a publisher to get funding.

A larger budget brings larger risks. By making the game faster and cheaper, we can set a price tag below the market average. A publisher likely wouldn't agree to that. Furthermore, our game touches on controversial religious themes. With outside investment, we would lose part of our creative freedom and be forced to smooth over the edges.

AI allows us to build the project exactly as we see it without needing outside investment. And, of course, this helps us keep the game's future price from going up.

3. Game quality and player experience come first.

We treat AI as a productivity tool. It makes up only a small part of our pipeline, where it performs exceptionally well. No generation makes it through without manual refinement.

We communicate with the community in our Discord via the #AI-artifact-hunters channel. There, the community points out small AI mistakes that made it into the demo, and we quickly fix and polish them.

Now, about the pipelines: where and how we actually use AI.

2D Art

Characters

  • It all starts with an idea and references, which are turned into a prompt (sometimes using reference drafts) and sent to a neural network. For style references, we use already finalized art assets from the game. Long iterations with AI lead us to a result that satisfies us all, or to a rethinking of the idea if we can't make something cool and high-quality.
  • Next, in Photoshop (without AI), we refine the generated art, paint in the details we want to see, and clean it of artifacts.
  • Then, again, manually in Photoshop, we cut the art into layers and paint what’s underneath them.
  • We import the finished layers into Spine, where we create animations manually without any AI help.
  • Finally, we manually integrate the character into Unity and set it up.

Cards and Items

  • The process is the same, except we don’t need to cut them into layers and animate.

3D Art

  • AI is not involved here. Map rooms, battlefields, and everything else are modeled, rigged, and assembled manually. We use other ways to optimize content creation, such as the Unity Asset Store and assets from our previous game, so AI doesn't provide any speedup here.

Shaders and VFX

  • The same goes here: no AI, everything is done by hand.

Code

  • We use Cursor, which generates a significant amount of code. It handles variations of things already in the project very well, like pop-ups, new abilities, monsters, cards, and so on.

UI and Icons

  • Everything is drawn and assembled exclusively by hand.

Animations

  • All the animations you see in the game are made manually without the use of AI.

Localization

  • We use AI for quick localization and then fix it based on community feedback. Before the release, we will hire professional translators to give the game a truly high-quality translation.

Music and Sounds

  • All music and sounds in the game are created manually. We might use AI to create music or sounds for our marketing materials, but it won’t leak into the game itself. We believe that music is crucial for providing the right player experience.

Game Design

  • We might ask a neural network for advice on which biblical plot an item could be linked to, or analyze reviews of other games to better understand communities' needs, but the final decision is always ours.

I hope that now that you clearly understand how we use AI, it will be easier to accept its presence in the project. This is the only way our small team can release truly fast and high-quality updates for our demo.

If you have any questions, we’ll be happy to discuss them in our Discord!

See you soon!
